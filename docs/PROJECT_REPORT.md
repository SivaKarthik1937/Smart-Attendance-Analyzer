# Project Report — Smart Attendance Pattern Analyzer

## 1. Objective

Build an end-to-end AI-powered attendance analytics platform that:
- Predicts a student's attendance **risk level** (Low / Medium / High)
- **Forecasts** a student's projected semester-end attendance percentage
- **Segments** students into behavioural groups (Excellent / Average / Critical)
- Surfaces these insights through role-appropriate dashboards for students and faculty, with exportable reports

This report covers the dataset, methodology, model results, and key design decisions made throughout the ML lifecycle and the full-stack implementation.

---

## 2. Dataset

A synthetic dataset was generated (`backend/dataset/generate_dataset.py`) rather than sourced from a real institution, for privacy and reproducibility. Realism was prioritized over pure randomness:

- **5,000 students**, 6 departments, 8 semesters, 30 subjects → **22,535 student-subject attendance records**
- Each student has a hidden "diligence" factor (Beta distribution, right-skewed) driving correlated behaviour across attendance, leaves, and internal marks
- A behavioural **trend** (improving / stable / declining) is assigned per student and used to generate a genuine 6-month monthly attendance series (not just noise)
- **Risk labels are rule-based** (a weighted score combining attendance level, trend, leave count, and internal marks), not random — this ensures the classifiers have real, learnable signal rather than fitting noise

**Validation performed on the generated data:**

| Check | Result |
|---|---|
| Mean attendance % | 73.7% (right-skewed, realistic) |
| Risk label balance | 47.3% Low / 26.7% Medium / 25.9% High |
| Correlation: Attendance vs Internal Marks | +0.82 |
| Correlation: Attendance vs Leaves Taken | -0.93 |
| Correlation: Attendance vs Distance from College | -0.10 (weak, as expected) |

## 3. Model 1 — Attendance Risk Classification

**Task:** Multi-class classification (Low / Medium / High Risk)
**Features:** current attendance %, previous semester attendance %, leaves taken, internal marks, distance from college, last month's attendance %
**Algorithms compared:** Decision Tree vs. Random Forest

| Model | Accuracy | Precision | Recall | F1 | Macro ROC-AUC |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Decision Tree | 96.09% | 96.10% | 96.09% | 96.10% | 0.9948 |
| Random Forest (selected) | 96.80% | 96.81% | 96.80% | 96.81% | 0.9973 |

Random Forest was automatically selected (highest weighted F1) and persisted. Feature importance analysis confirmed `current_attendance` and `leaves_taken` dominate the model's decisions — consistent with how the dataset's risk labels were constructed, which is a meaningful sanity check that the model learned genuine structure rather than spurious correlations.

---

## 4. Model 2 — Attendance Percentage Forecast

**Task:** Regression — predict projected semester-end attendance %
**Target construction:** To avoid trivial leakage (predicting current attendance from itself), the target was built by fitting a real linear trend across each student-subject's 6-month attendance series and extrapolating it 2 months forward with added noise — a genuine, trend-grounded forecast.
**Features:** current attendance, previous attendance, leaves, internal marks, distance, and an inference-friendly `trend_rate` proxy: (current - previous) / 6
**Algorithms compared:** Linear Regression vs. Random Forest Regressor

| Model | MAE | RMSE | R2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Linear Regression | 2.398 | 3.037 | 0.9624 |
| Random Forest Regressor (selected) | 2.361 | 3.025 | 0.9627 |

Both models perform very similarly (the underlying relationship is close to linear), with Random Forest selected by a small margin on R2.

## 5. Model 3 — Student Segmentation

**Task:** Unsupervised clustering into Excellent / Average / Critical
**Features (student-level aggregates):** average attendance, average leaves, average internal marks, distance from college
**Algorithm:** K-Means (k=3), features standardized before clustering; clusters mapped to human labels by ranking on mean attendance

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Silhouette Score | 0.334 |
| Excellent | 38.7% of students |
| Average | 40.3% of students |
| Critical | 21.0% of students |

A silhouette score of ~0.33 is reasonable (not artificially perfect) for continuous, correlated human-behaviour features — real clusters in this kind of data are rarely cleanly separated, and an inflated score would suggest overly simplistic synthetic data.

## 6. AI Recommendation Engine

Rather than using a fourth ML model (which would be opaque and harder to justify to end users), recommendations are generated by a transparent, rule-based engine (`ml/predict.py::generate_ai_recommendations`) that combines the outputs of all three models:
- Attendance threshold warnings (below 75%, trending toward 75%)
- Trend-based encouragement or caution
- Risk-level-specific advice (e.g. "high risk of detention -- speak with your advisor")
- Leave-count and internal-marks correlation nudges
- Segment-specific framing
- Subject-specific advice (flagging the single weakest subject)

This keeps the "why" behind every recommendation auditable -- a deliberate choice given this is student-facing, potentially high-stakes advice.

## 7. Full-Stack Design Decisions

- **Backend/frontend contract kept in sync manually but deliberately**: `frontend/src/types/index.ts` mirrors `backend/models.py` 1:1, making drift immediately visible during development.
- **Worst-case risk aggregation for faculty views**: a student is flagged at their highest-severity risk across any single subject, not an average -- the conservative, actionable choice for an early-warning system.
- **Precomputed analytics over live recomputation**: correlation matrices and EDA statistics are computed once via `analysis/eda.py` rather than on every dashboard load, keeping the Analytics page fast at scale.
- **Role-based route guards on the frontend** (`App.tsx`) mirror the backend's `require_student`/`require_faculty` dependencies, so unauthorized views are never even rendered, not just blocked at the API layer.
- **Design system ("Academic Ledger")**: a deliberately non-generic visual identity (deep navy + ledger-gold, Fraunces/Inter/IBM Plex Mono, a signature proportional Risk Ledger Strip) rather than default Tailwind/shadcn styling, chosen to reflect the academic/gradebook subject matter.

## 8. Known Limitations / Future Work

- The dataset is synthetic; real institutional data would likely have messier, less cleanly-correlated features and would need re-validation of the risk-label thresholds.
- There is no dedicated "edit my faculty profile" endpoint yet -- the Profile page shows faculty a leaner view than students for this reason (documented honestly in the code rather than papered over).
- The forecast model's `trend_rate` feature is a cross-semester proxy at inference time, while the target was built from real within-semester monthly slopes during training -- a reasonable approximation, but a real deployment with actual monthly attendance logging could tighten this.
- No automated test suite (unit/integration tests) was included in this build; given the scope, correctness was validated by actually running the dataset generator, all three training scripts, and static analysis (syntax + cross-module import checks) on every backend/frontend file instead.
